title: Marriage is Overrated
---
Yup, that is my hot take: marriage is a bogus concept. Note that if you are happily married or intend to marry your significant other, that is awesome, and I am happy for you. But to me, I don't think there is the necessity to get married in the first place.

Imagine living in a world without the concept of marriage. Two lovebirds start to hang out, fall in love with each other, and all of a sudden, one of them proposes (no pun intended): 'I really enjoy hanging out with you; let's continue to do that, until either the day I die or you die.' If I'm not mistaken, this is essentially the literal definition of marriage: a contract that lasts a lifetime.

Here is the caveat: what is the point of 'hanging out until death,' besides tax benefits and having kids, especially in this economy? You might argue that marriage is about commitment and all, but there is commitment in a romantic relationship as well (at least there should be). Not to mention the fact that people still cheat during a marriage regardless.

I think at the end of the day, people want to get married because they want to have control in their life. The one thing that can make them feel like they have control is to lock down that one person they love dearly. Marriage, by its definition, affords them the possibility to spend a whole life with another person and that gives them hope.

At this point, you might wonder who hurt me and call me hopeless, but I'm just not sold on the idea of marriage yet. Maybe it's because I haven't found my special someone, but I think there is something more worthwhile than marriage.
